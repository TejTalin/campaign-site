The Plan

What I Will Do

Five strategic pillars to transform how BMU plans, runs, and remembers its Eventss.

01
The Legacy Vault
A centralised digital repository of SOPs, vendor databases, and After-Action Reports. Flagship Eventss like Milestone X and Melange grow every year instead of restarting from zero.
02
Niche Events Renaissance
Reviving lost campus traditions Halloween Nights, Open-Air Cinema, Themed Proms ensuring a vibrant social calendar beyond the major fests.
03
Handover Protocol
A structured institutional memory system so successful Events knowledge is passed down. High standards maintained regardless of leadership changes.
04
Phased Budget Roadmap
Quarterly fund distribution to ensure smaller club-led and seasonal Eventss are never cancelled due to budget depletion mid-year.
05
Zero-Waste Vendor Model
Semester-long contracts with verified vendors eliminate last-minute markups. Savings redirected to better artists, sound, and production quality.
06
External Sponsorships
Active relationship-building with external partners and sponsors to subsidise Eventss premium experiences without straining the student fund.
07
The Mosaic Calendar
Overhauling regional and religious celebrations Pongal, Baisakhi, Onam, Eid with regional student consultants for authentic decor, food, and performances.
08
Stealth Awareness Eventss
Mental Health Acoustic Jams, Sustainability Thrift Markets, Social Equity Dialogues moving important topics from the classroom to the campus ground.
09
BMU's Got Talent
A flagship talent discovery programme in collaboration with campus clubs a massive stage for our student artists, performers, and creators to shine.
10
48-Hour Greenlight
A fast-track approval framework for student-led micro-Eventss. If the plan is safe and solid, administrative clearance within 48 hours keeping campus active daily.
11
Club Concierge Hub
Acting as dedicated liaison between clubs and administration. I handle the logistical red tape so club leads focus entirely on creativity and execution.
12
Volunteer Empowerment Network
A structured student volunteer corps with clear roles and rewards. On-ground execution for central Eventss that's seamless and professional.
13
Prime-Time Programming
Prioritising "Flash Eventss" during common break hours so every student including those with tight schedules or transportation constraints can participate fully.
14
Feedback Town Halls
Open feedback sessions twice every semester. Events execution refined through real-time student input not assumptions about what works.
15
Inter-University Synergy
A Fest Outreach Network to inform our community about other universities' fests and encourage BMU participation healthy, competitive engagement.
